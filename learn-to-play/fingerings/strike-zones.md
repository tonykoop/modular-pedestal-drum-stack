# Strike Zones

Each drum has 3 useful strike zones:

```
   .--.
  /    \
 |  C   |      C = center  → fundamental (target pitch); loudest, cleanest tone
 |  O   |      O = 1/3 off-center → first overtone (splash, no clear pitch)
 |  R   |      R = rim → shell ring mode (percussive accent, no pitch)
  \    /
   '--'
```

## Center strike

- Mallet contact point: dead centre of the head
- Tone: fundamental, full resonance
- Use for: melodic pentatonic phrases

## 1/3 off-center

- Mallet contact point: between centre and rim, at 1/3 of the radius from
  centre (about 50 mm in on drum-1 down to 17 mm on drum-5)
- Tone: first overtone dominates — non-harmonic with the fundamental
- Use for: percussive fills, ghosting, "splash" texture

## Rim shot

- Mallet contact point: on the bearing edge / counter hoop
- Tone: shell ring mode (1.6 kHz on drum-1, 4.9 kHz on drum-5)
- Use for: accents, click track

## Mallet selection

- **Soft felt** (Vic Firth T1) — warm, melodic, recommended for most phrases
- **Hard felt** — brighter, more cut
- **Wood tip** — sharpest attack, useful for drum-5's higher frequencies

Avoid metal-tipped mallets — they'll dent the heads quickly.
