# Pentatonic Warm-ups

Drums numbered 1 (largest, A2) through 5 (smallest, A3).

## Warm-up 1 — Ascending and descending

```
1 - 2 - 3 - 4 - 5 - 4 - 3 - 2 - 1
A2 C3  E3  G3  A3  G3  E3  C3  A2
```

Quarter notes at 80 BPM, single-stroke alternating hands. Goal: even tone and
even timing, not speed.

## Warm-up 2 — Pentatonic thirds

```
1 - 3 - 2 - 4 - 3 - 5 - 4 - 1
A2 E3  C3 G3 E3 A3  G3 A2
```

## Warm-up 3 — Roll on each drum

Sustained roll (sixteenth notes) on drum-1 for 4 beats, then drum-2, then
drum-3, etc. Tests dynamic control at different sizes.

## Warm-up 4 — Accent on the root

```
*1 - 2 - 3 - 4 - 5 - 4 - 3 - 2
```

Accent (sf) on drum-1 each time; ghost notes (pp) on the others. Develops
accent-vs-ghost dynamics.

## Tune-up tap pattern

Cross-tap each drum's rim and adjust until tap pitches match around the rim
(see `tuning-notes.md`).
