# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A single vertical sheet-metal drum tower roughly chest-to-head tall, built as a column of five single-headed cylindrical steel drums sharing one slender central pedestal pole. The five matte-black rolled-steel shells step down in diameter from a broad drum at the bottom to a small one at the top, each shell shorter than the one below it, so the silhouette tapers upward like an ascending stair of cylinders. Each drum carries a single cream-white synthetic head on top, tensioned by a brass snare-style external lug ring with six tension rods radiating around the rim and a counter-hoop pulling the head down. The drums rise off a round walnut-toned weighted base plate with four small levelling feet at its rim and a centre socket gripping the foot of the thin polished stainless pole; small lapis-blue rubber pucks sit in the gaps between each drum, marking the vibration-isolation points. The metal shells show clean dressed weld seams and chamfered top bearing edges where head meets steel. Realistic floor-standing scale, a clean neutral studio background, soft directional studio light, 3/4 product-photography angle, ~50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

The pedestal drum tower staged in a working percussion studio or rehearsal room, standing on its own footprint like a floor tom but rising as a slim column of five stacked steel drums of descending size. A standing player holds a pair of mallets mid-phrase, reaching across the cream-white heads from the broad low drum to the small high one, body language showing fast melodic movement down and up the column. Warm natural light from a large window or soft-box, brass lug rings catching highlights against the matte-black shells, the lapis-blue isolation pucks just visible between drums, walnut base anchoring the frame. Shallow depth of field, environmental storytelling, concept visualization only.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of a single drum's tensioning hardware on the matte-black steel shell: one brass snare-style lug seated against the curved shell wall, its tension rod threading up through the counter-hoop, and the inverted-U claw gripping the flesh hoop of the cream-white synthetic head. The 45-degree inward-chamfered bearing edge where the head stretches over the rolled steel rim is visible, along with a faint dressed weld seam running down the shell. Raking side light rakes across the brass and brushed steel to bring out texture, shallow focus falloff into the curve of the shell, high detail, no measurements.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension callouts or measurement lines; no impossible mechanisms, no floating drums, no double heads on these single-headed shells; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
