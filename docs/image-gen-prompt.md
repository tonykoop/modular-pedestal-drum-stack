# Modular Pedestal Drum Stack Image-Gen Prompts

Concept visualization only; not fabrication authority. Do not infer shell
blank sizes, pole brackets, lug placement, or tuning from these prompts.

## Hero Prompt

Photorealistic 3/4 product photograph of a finished modular pedestal drum stack: five single-headed rolled sheet-steel drums mounted vertically around a central stainless pedestal pole, diameters stepping down from largest at the bottom to smallest at the top, with synthetic mylar batter heads, external snare-style lug rings, rubber isolation pucks between modules, and a heavy circular steel base. The instrument should read as a compact melodic tom tower, pentatonic-friendly, with brushed or matte steel shells and a pair of mallets nearby for scale. Clean studio background, soft directional lighting, realistic hardware, 50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic.

## In-Context Prompt

Photorealistic performance-workshop scene of the modular pedestal drum stack being played standing up with two mallets, the player moving vertically from the large lower drum to the small top drum. Include a sheet-metal shop or rehearsal space setting, tuning key, drum dial, rubber pucks, and a stable base visible at floor level. Natural light, believable ergonomic reach, no exaggerated stage effects.

## Detail / Macro Prompt

Photorealistic macro close-up of the pedestal stack's mounting mechanism: one rolled steel drum shell with a mylar head and lug ring, an internal or side bracket meeting the central stainless pole, a rubber isolation puck, and clean TIG-welded or bolted hardware. Show brushed steel, black rubber, chrome lugs, and real head collar texture. High-detail macro, shallow depth of field.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, floating drums, impossible pedestal physics, exposed sharp hardware, extra limbs, distorted mallets, or fake pitch labels.
