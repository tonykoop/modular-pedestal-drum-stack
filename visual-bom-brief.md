# Visual BOM Brief — Modular Pedestal Drum Stack

One paragraph for a future visual BOM render (e.g. exploded-view illustration or
infographic for the showcase).

---

Render an **exploded vertical stack** with the pedestal pole shown as a faint
dashed line at the centre. From bottom to top: a circular base plate (380 mm)
with 4 levelling feet at the rim and a centre socket holding the bottom of the
pole. Above that, five drum shells stack in descending diameter (300, 250,
200, 150, 100 mm) and decreasing height. Each shell shows its 6 lugs as small
satellite cylinders, with tension rods radiating outward. The counter hoops sit
just above each shell rim with the drum heads stretched between hoop and shell.
Between each pair of drums, a small rubber puck (red) marks the isolation point.
Colour palette: warm walnut for the base plate, cream-white for the heads,
matte-black for the shells (or natural steel if visible-weld finish), brass for
the lugs and rods, lapis-blue for rubber pucks. Background: muted cream
gradient. Mood: precise, archival, with the feel of an Eames-era furniture
catalogue. Annotation callouts label each drum with its target pitch (A2, C3,
E3, G3, A3). Total render size: 3000 × 4500 px for retina display.
